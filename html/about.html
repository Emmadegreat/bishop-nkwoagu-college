About Us

The School started on 9th September 2018 and the pioneer Manager is Ven Engr Kenechukwu Obinna & Mrs Ngozi Rose Onyemaobim who is serving as the Vicar from 13th January 2017 till date. Like a mustard seed the School started with very few students is growing to the glory of God. The Pioneer and the serving Principal is in the person of Mrs Tina Ahawike. She was posted to the new School by His Lordship Rt Rev Monday Chukwuma Nkwoagu JP to serve as the Principal.
The pioneer teachers include Mr Chukwuma Mgbebu, Chidinma Oluebube Onyemaobim, Nmesoma Chiamaka Onyemaobim, Edwin Okechukwu a Corps Member, an Ivorian by name; Lawrence who was handling the French classes, Pastor Nnamdi Ihetu, Mrs Riches Efeomo now the Dean of Studies. Mrs Eucharia Onyebuchi latter joined the teaching crew. The Teaching Crew had since grown in number and qualifications overtime. The pioneer Senior Prefect of the School is in the person of Master Izuchukwu Israel Nwafor.

In 2020 a Church Education Committee was constituted with Mr Chukwudi Uchenna Onwudiwe as the Committee Chairman, Dr Celestine Ukwu Nweke as the Secretary of the Committee, Hon Barr Kelechi Mbam, Prof Mrs Ann Nwigboji, Mrs Victoria Echefu, and Mrs Ndidi Okeke, as members.
It is worthy of note that our school is an approved center for external examinations.

For excellence in learning the School has well developed standard Science Laboratories to enhance studies by practical experiments. School Library is well developed and equipped. The school currently has fourteen (14) classrooms, Principalâ€™s Office, a Staff Room, an Office for the Dean of Studies, an Office for the School Counselor, a 300-seat capacity hall for examinations and other school activities.
For a holistic learning module, the school has many sports facilities including Mini Football Field, Basket Ball Posts, Badminton Court, Volley Ball facilities and Table Tennis facilities are already in place. All these we believe to help our students blend their academic activities with extra-curriculum activities, because all work no play will make Jack a dull boy.

OUR RULES AND REGULATIONS

The mission statement of every good Mission Educational Institution remains inculcating in students sound morals, training them in both character and learning, therefore the following rules and regulations have been stipulated to guide both the Teachers and students.

General Rules For Students

  1. Any student found advertising use of Alcohol, hard drugs etc within the School Premises will be expelled from the School.
  2. Wrong dressing code is not allowed. The School dress code must be adhered to. Short skirts, Bedroom slippers, and long dangling earrings are not allowed. Full stop type of earrings is recommended. Unkempt hairstyle is not allowed.
  3. Any student who carelessly destroys any school property will replace same.
  4. Cultism is highly prohibited and any evidence of cultism earns the victim expulsion.
  5. Students should not in any manner or for any reason bully one another. Physical and/or verbal violence within the school premises is not allowed. Any student caught in this act will wash all the toilet ends for one (1) week.
  6. Fighting among students is punishable.
  7. Any student caught in theft will be suspended for one (1) week and upon resumption should write a letter of Apology to school copied to the Management which he/she will read out in the School General Assembly.
  8. Students should not be disrespectful to the teachers and the school Authority and all other elders. Students are not allowed to engage in war of words with the teachers and should not by any means insult the teachers. This will attract one (1) week suspension with a letter of apology to the teacher and School Authority.
  9. Possession or use of Mobile Phones and other electronic gadgets such as RADIO, TOYS, and HEADPHONE etc are prohibited. Any student caught will be made to bring his/her parents for explanations. With no justified explanations the gadget will be permanently confiscated by the School Authority and the student suspended.
  10. Student should shun examination malpractices. If caught the culprit will be punished.
  11. Lateness to school is not allowed and defaulters will be punished.
  12. Student must attend classes with recommended textbooks, notebooks including home work, pen, and pencil.
  13. On no account should any student leave the school premises before dismissal without express permission.
  14. Leaving the classroom without exit and Loitering is not allowed.
  15. Littering the classroom and the compound is not allowed.
  16. Vandalism is not allowed. Note that lockers and stools issued to students serve as palliatives and encouragement to the parents and guardians.
    a. Therefore any form vandalism on these and other school properties will attract fine of Ten Thousand Naira only which must be paid before the student is allowed to attend classes and write exams.
    b. Any class that intentionally mutilates the painted classroom walls will repurchase the paint and pay the painter to redo the sections affected.
    c. Any student that intentionally breaks the Alumaco Window glass will replace the same.
  17. Careless and wasteful use of water is highly prohibited.
  18. Attendance to School Assembly is compulsory for all students.
  19. Students must attend assembly with complete recommended Assembly materials.
  20. Entering classrooms outside school hours and during holidays is not allowed. Any student caught will be held responsible for any loss or damages observed during such periods.

Library

  1. Students should not make any form of noise in and around the library.
  2. Students are not allowed to take any copy of library books out of the library except on borrowing arrangement.
  3. Students should not attempt to replace books on the shelves after reading. The Library Attendant does that.
  4. On no account should any Students tear out any part or page of any library Book.

Laboratory

  1. Laboratories should not be used as private study room.
  2. Movements that may cause damages to laboratory equipments must be avoided.
  3. Pilfering away laboratory items and equipments attracts serious punishment.
  4. Appropriate dress code should be observed at all times.
  5. Students must wear their lab coats while attending Laboratory practical classes.

For staff

  1. Teachers should always be punctual to school and to classes.
  2. Teachers are allowed to look unkempt.
  3. Teachers should nit have any sexual relationship with the students. Such act will automatically earn the teacher termination of his/her appointment.
  4. Teachers should not for any reason loose control in front of the students.
  5. Teachers should not make fun of the students.
  6. Teachers should not bully the students.
  7. Teacher s should not use abusive word s on the students.
  8. Teachers should not humiliate, insult or fight his/her colleague.
